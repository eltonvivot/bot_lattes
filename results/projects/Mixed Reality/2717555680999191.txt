2008 - Atual
DCR-PGR proposal linked to NSF granted project entitled ?TRMS: Ionome to the Genome: Mapping the Gene Networks Controlling Nutrient Content in Rice Grain?
Descrição: Rice is a traditional food crop in Brazil, and is the major source of carbohydrates in Brazilian diet.  Despite the fact that Brazil is among the ten major world rice producers, Brazilian rice production is not enough to satisfy the domestic demand. In 2007, Brazil?s harvested rice area was 3.0 million hectares. While total rice area in Brazil has decreased over the past 20 years, continual increases in yield have allowed production to remain relatively stable. Rice yields have steadily increased over the last 20 years due to improved technology (e.g. seed varieties, management practices, increased irrigation). Nonetheless Brazil?s stocks have been continually decreasing over the last few years, mainly due to non-proportional increases between demand and production.
Nutritional anemia derived from iron deficiency is widespread among rice-consuming countries, and could have severe impacts in child health. In Brazil, 50% of children at pre-school age have shown this type of anemia. The problem becomes worse at low income populations, mainly small farmers who produce their own rice. This fact makes food biofortification strategies with little or no impact in this context. In this scenario, development of new varieties of rice richer in bio-available iron is considered an important topic of plant biotechnology strategies for the developing world.

The activities proposed here to occur in collaboration with the laboratories of the Dr. David Salt, Dr. Mary Lou Guerinot involves the use of equipments or expertise not available in our research groups, and includes research activities complementary to other ones already in course in our labs. Whereas in the Brazilian side, this collaboration will improve the research quality and data produced, the American research teams will benefit from new rice variability, and from contrasting environmental conditions important for QTL identification and contributions.
