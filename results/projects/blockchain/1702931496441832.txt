2013 - 2015
Towards a framework to investigate the relationship between Improvisation & Agility Theory
Descrição: This research project aims to further explore the ?agility theoretical model? to better understand the relationship between the ?agility concept? and the ?improvisation concept? under the project management perspective in dynamic and innovative business environments. In this research the ?improvisation concept? is being analyzed as a team's capability and how it correlates with the agility performance and the project performance and results. This project is a part of the work conducted by Dr. Edivandro Conforto at MIT. The theoretical model is going to be tested and empirically verified through case studies and/or survey.. 2013 - 2015
Exploring the relationship between the Agility Construct and Project and Product Performance - a global survey
Descrição: We are uncovering the relationship between management practices, organizational factors, the agility construct and the project and product performance. This research project has been entitled as the "Project Management Agility Global Survey". The results and findings of this research will be used to validate the theoretical framework and will serve as the basis to build and enhance the "agility theory" focused on the project management and product development areas. Once the framework is considered valid and coherent we will be able to advance the ?agility theory? by investigating how to develop prescriptive models to help companies in the identification of the most suitable management practices according to the organizational environment and the project type in order to boost the team?s agility performance as well as project and product success.. 2012 - 2015
Improving Integration of Program Management and Systems Engineering
Descrição: For many years, a cultural barrier has existed between practitioners of systems engineering and of program management. Some systems engineers and program managers have developed the mindset that their work activities are separate from each other rather than part of an organic whole. Consequently, work often costs more, takes longer, and provides a suboptimal solution for the customer or end user. The leaders of INCOSE and PMI believe this cultural barrier and mindset can and must be overcome. By working together, the organizations hope to foster a team approach that will benefit their members and their organizations, and ultimately the stakeholders who depend on them.
INCOSE, PMI and MIT plan to use the survey findings to raise awareness of the implications of that cultural barrier to practitioners of both disciplines and to uncover potential areas of collaboration between the three organizations. CEPE?s complimentary research efforts will help develop new knowledge, insights, and tools that enable better integration and outcomes in engineering programs. Together, they will help to create a new mindset. The new mindset is the understanding that both disciplines are two interlocking pieces of a puzzle. When they are separate, only partial views of reality can be seen. Only when they are synergistically brought together can the larger picture become clear, and the puzzle can be solved.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. Alunos envolvidos: Doutorado: (1) . Integrantes: Edivandro Carlos Conforto - Coordenador / Eric Rebentisch - Integrante / Monica Rossi - Integrante / Josef Oehmen - Integrante / Maria Pacenza - Integrante.

2009 - 2013
Modelo para Avaliação da Agilidade de Práticas de Gestão de Projetos em Empresas Inovadoras
Descrição: Projeto de pesquisa de doutorado financiado pela FAPESP. O objetivo é desenvolver um modelo para avaliação da agilidade no gerenciamento de projetos de inovação. Como resultado pretende-se desenvolver a "Teoria da Agilidade" como uma competência e fundamentação teórica que possa ser útil para explicar a relação entre práticas de gestão e fatores críticos de diversas naturezas e o desempenho em agilidade do processo de gerenciamento de projetos. Uma vez compreendido esta relação de causa e efeito, o estudo poderá focar na relação entre desempenho em agilidade e o desempenho em inovação e melhores resultados dos projetos de novos produtos.. 2007 - 2009
Gerenciamento Ágil de Projetos: proposta e avaliação de método para gestão de escopo e tempo.
Descrição: Projeto de pesquisa cujo objetivo é desenvolver, implantar e avaliar um método para gestão de escopo e tempo voltado para o desenvolvimento de produtos inovadores para aplicação em empresas de base tecnológica. O método intitulado de IVPM2 (Iterative and Visual Project Management Method) combina conceitos e melhores práticas de desenvolvimento de produtos e gestão de projetos com princípios da gestão ágil de projetos (Agile Project Management). Apresenta uma avaliação do desempenho do método nas empresas participantes da pesquisa.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa.
