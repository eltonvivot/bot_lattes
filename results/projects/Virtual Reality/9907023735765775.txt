RaioSat - Total Lightning Flash Detection from Space: A CubeSat Approach
Descrição: The RaioSat project intends to detect intra-cloud and cloud-to-ground lightning flashes simultaneously, the so-called total lightning data, using an optical sensor and a VHF antenna onboard a CubeSat platform. Two different sensor networks that detect and locate lightning flashes in Brazil, called RINDAT and BrasilDAT, will be used as reference data. The RaioSat mission is expected to be in a LEO orbit at 650km and it will use a 3U-CubeSat aluminum frame (10x10x30cm) to accommodate the main platform and its payload. The main platform shall have telemetry, commanding and housekeeping capabilities via an on-board computer, 3-axis attitude control and a GPS. The payload shall have a VHF passive antenna (range of 50 to 200MHz) and a spectral imaging camera (SIC) with resolution of 2,048 x 1,536 pixels leading to a surface imaging of 80 m/pixel. Also SIC shall have a spectral range from 700 to 900nm using a band-pass optical filter. Additionally, the paper briefly describes all the main stages of the space mission over the system life-cycle.. Situação: Em andamento; Natureza: Pesquisa. Development of a Langmuir probe to be launched on board a nano satellite Cubesat
Descrição: The project envisages the development of a scientific experiment, a Langmuir Probe (SLP)to be flown on board one of the nano satellites CubeSat for making in-situ measurements of the ionospheric plasma density and temperature along the trajectory of the CubeSat. The main steps of the project are:· Development of a new engineering model, compacted with miniature components of a Langmuir Probe, already flown on board sounding rockets and on board the micro satellite SACI-1.· Development and qualification of an interface between the scientific experiment and the on board computer of the CubeSat for storing and preprocessing of the scientific data from the experiment.· Fabrication of the Qualifiaction and or the Prototype and Flight Models of the experiment.
