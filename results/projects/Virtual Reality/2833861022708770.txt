The nutritional ecology of insect larvae: focus on major agricultural pest species
Descrição: This project aims at understanding the patterns of feeding behaviour and growth of insect larvae. In particular, we use the standard model organism Drosophila melanogaster as well as the major Australian agricultural pest Bactrocera tryoni. We use concepts of the Geometric Framework of Nutrition (Nutritional Geometry) to investigate how larvae choose their food, and the fitness implications of these choices to growth and development.. Gum, germs and geometry: shaping the influence of nutrition on oral health
Descrição: Geometry class. Very few thoughts in mind, none of them related to actual problem-solving. Generally, the thoughts are related to friends, parties and food. It turns out that that food and geometry can be linked in more ways than we previously expected. This project uses a state-of-the-art method in nutritional research, the Geometric Framework (GF), to explore the effects of nutrient intake on the development of gum disease. The aim is to move beyond the traditional view of sugar as the villain of oral health, disentangling the role of macronutrients on the onset and progression of periodontitis. Although painful, geometry can teach us a great deal about ourselves and our nutritional habits, and can help us improve the treatment and prevention of oral diseases.. Let?s talk innovation: leading oral research with an insect model
Descrição: Insects and oral health: who?d have thought? Even though insects seem to lack any structure that remotely resembles a tooth, these animals can serve as models for bacteria infections and inflammatory responses. In this project, we aim to harness the powerful model organism Drosophila melanogaster to investigate how infections by the oral bacteria can modulate immunological responses. Using a combination of D. melanogaster mutants for immune genes and strains of healthy and pathogenic oral bacteria, we aim to obtain new insights on the molecular mechanisms by which the oral bacteria infect and thrive against the immune system.  This innovative project opens a new avenue of research in oral health and insect physiology by bridging two seemingly distinct fields of science.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. 2017 - 2018
Vascular effects on an experimental model of periodontitis
Descrição: ?Parts of a whole?. Today more than ever science understands the meaning of these words in general health. Every day, more evidences reveal the implications of local responses to environmental challenges (e.g. infections) to the overall equilibrium of the organism?s physiology. For instance, recent findings suggest that local inflammation in the gums ? also known as periodontitis ? may increase the blocking of blood vessels leading to strokes and death. But how can inflammatory responses in the oral cavity influence cardiovascular and general health? Addressing this unanswered question is the purpose of this project, which has two main goals: i) to establish a mice model of periodontitis, whereby the inflammatory responses in the gum can be produced and replicated successfully, and ii) to unravel the molecular mechanisms linking periodontitis and cardiovascular disease.  The outcomes of this project are likely to affect not only basic research, but also clinical preventive care and public health.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. 2016 - 2019
Gut microbiota and its implications for mate choice in Drosophila melanogaster
Descrição: Gut microbiota influences a range of behavioural and physiological traits across the animal kingdom. Yet, little is known about its implications to mate choice and sexual behaviour. This project aims to unravel the effects of gut microbiota manipulation on mate choice of males and females Drosophila melanogaster. In addition, the project aims to reveal the underlying molecular mechanisms by which gut microbiota exert its effects. For instance, the project is divided into two main branches: i) the behavioural responses (i.e. mate choice and mating investment) of individuals of both sexes to changes in the gut microbiota composition; ii) the molecular mechanism such as pheromone production and immune system activation that may underlie the behavioural choices.. 2016 - 2017
Bacteria and gingiva: friends or foes?
Descrição: Adequate oral health is a major concern in the 21st century. According to the World Health Organization, oral diseases affect ~90% of the world population, amongst which the most prevalent diseases are cavity and gum disease. Interestingly, both diseases are an outcome of the disruption of the composition and function of the oral microorganisms (i.e. ?dysbiosis?), which in turn influence the interactions with oral environment including the tooth surface and the gingiva. Although key microorganisms are known to be associated with the onset and severity of diseases (e.g. Porphyromonas gingivalis in gum inflammation), the mechanisms by which the microorganisms interact with the oral environment remain largely overlooked. This gap hinders our understanding on the implications of an impaired oral health to the physiological equilibrium of the organism. To address this problem, this project uses cutting-edge techniques of metabolomics in conjunction with cell culture and in vitro bacteria-cell interactions to investigate how the oral bacteria affect gingival epithelia cells. The aim of this project is to establish an in vitro system to test the conditions that favours the prevalence and severity of oral bacteria infections. Given the high prevalence of oral diseases caused by oral bacteria, the findings of this research are likely to be of wide interest in the fields of dentistry, medicine and public health..
