Ontogenetic patterning of the acellular endolymphatic calcium deposits and bone formation of Thoropa miliaris: A X-ray diffraction study
Descrição: Projeto XRD1 - 20180534. As part of a serie of studies on the morphology of the Brazilian species Thoropa miliaris, it is known that tadpoles of this species accumulate calcium carbonate crystals in their lymphatic glands, such structures are known as endolymphatic calcium deposits (ECD).These structures are composed of aragonite, a form of calcium carbonate and likely represent an important source os calcium for bone formation during and after metamorphosis. The development of the vertebrate skeleton reflect its evolutionary history. In the current project, it is expected to evaluate crystal structure of the endolymphatic calcium deposits, bone ? which are important for the understanding of larval evolution of Thoropa miliaris ? and sand consumed by tadpole for each development stage.
