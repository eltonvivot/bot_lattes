Measurement-based  control with applications
Descrição: Traditional engineering design is largely model based. This means that a mathematical model is required before any design approaches or design calculations can be initiated. In recent years, an alternative model-free measurement based approach effective for designing new control elements to be added to an existing unknown system has been developed. In the present proposal, we describe measurement based approaches to one important broad area of control engineering and to two important application areas. The control engineering is Robust control  and the application areas are the methods of searching for   maximum power of photovoltaic (PV) arrays and  the design of droop control strategies for microgrids.
Robustness is important in closed-loop control systems, since they must function despite perturbations in the parameters from those assumed in the design process. In this proposal, we describe an  approach for designing robust controllers based on frequency response measurements. The preliminary results  show how classical stability margin based designs can be upgraded to a computer aided approach showing achievable designs, through the analytic determination of design surfaces that qualitatively and quantitatively display design trade offs. This approach to design will be extended to other criteria such as control signal constraints and parameter uncertainty. 
The design of photovoltaic arrays presents an important practical problem where automatic determination of maximum power is desired under different environmental conditions. The technical challenge is made more difficult by the fact that PV cells have nonlinear V-I characteristics that change unpredictably with the solar incidence. We outline an approach based on pointwise Thévenin equivalents of the nonlinear PV array which can be determined from measurements. This  approach  can lead to an online measurement based algorithm to extract the maximum power from the PV arrays.

The design of droop control strategies for  power sharing in microgrids presents several challenges as the utility grid changes according to the occurrence of events such as connection/disconnection of the grid and sources. We outline an approach to the problem of power sharing based on measurements.
We propose to develop these two design approaches, which will  be evaluated in  an experimental test bed.. Situação: Em andamento; Natureza: Pesquisa. National Institute of Science and Technology for Cooperative Autonomous Systems Applied to Security and Environment
Descrição: This proposal aims the creation of a National Institute of Science and Technology (INCT-SAC) to deal with cooperative autonomous systems. Motivated by Brazil?s large territory and population size, wideranging geographic features, and economic and social issues, we intend to solve problems related to security and environment. This INCT will address some of these challenges by proposing novel networked robotic systems for underwater maintenance, automobile and cargo transportation, grow of crops, remote inspection and actuation, and surveillance. Oil extraction in the pre-salt layer, located 250Km from the coast and more then 2km deep in the ocean, has demanded specific solutions in terms of logistics, maintenance in deep waters, remote operations of platforms, and national security; Urban mobility has been faced with an expressive growing in the number of automobiles in the last years, the Brazilian car fleet is now the 4th in the world. Car manufacturers and robotics researchers have been interested in partial or full automation of vehicles navigation systems; In terms of Amazon rainforest and agriculture, Brazil has two important and complementary objectives in terms of preservation and to increase crop productivity. Advances in communication, computing, and embedded control technologies allow the application of networked systems to large environmental monitoring and coordination of multiple mobile agents for exploration and manipulation. The proposed INCT will bring together an outstanding set of Universities, Industries, and Research Institutes of Brazil and abroad. The latest advances in systems theory and emerging information technology to enable the design and implementation of distributed networks for observation, mobility, and manipulation provide the fundamental scope of actual problems we intend to solve. Specifically, this proposal will consider autonomous navigation of aerial vehicles in low altitude to deal, for instance, with power transmission line and gas pipe inspection to alleviate the pilot?s workload and with motion planning strategies for aggressive landing of an actual cargo transportation helicopter in a mobile platform; Robotic systems based on lighter-than-air vehicles for environmental monitoring, surveillance and communication applied to the Amazon region. They will provide a virtual sensory barriers against deforestation, telecommunication services to remote and small population areas, technological solutions to coastal communities and remote settlements; Modeling and design of autonomous heavy vehicles with on-line payload measurement system. Advanced driver assistance systems will be developed to deal with safety, congestion and traffic cooperation, and with overloading freight transportation; Coordination of heterogeneous aerial and mobile robots in outdoor environment to deal with helicopters and convoy of trucks based on fault tolerant control systems; Coordination of heterogeneous robots applied to orange plantation monitoring. This project aims to estimate the amount of fruits, pest detection, the rate of development and degree of maturity of the plants; Modeling and design of distributed underwater vehicles for inspection and service of industrial installations. They will deal with servicing, repair, and assembly tasks of oil and gas platforms. Basic development for autonomous and fault tolerant control systems. It will be related with development of control techniques for systems under stochastic variations in its dynamics, with the development of measurement based approach to engineering design and techniques for system identification, and with control of small robots described by swarms of insects which will be performed based on bio-inspired systems.. 2014 - 2016
PVE project: A new model free, measurement based approach to design: applications to circuits, power and control systems
Descrição: An important challenging problem in modern engineering is the development of effective ways to deal with large scale, high order and complex systems. The classical approach to the engineering of complex systemshas been model based, namely that models of increasing complexity and high dynamic order are produced as a first step in the analysis, synthesis and design cycle. This trend has been spurred by advances in computer technology which promised fast computation and design optimization of complex systems. However in many fields including Circuits, Control and Power Systems the high order and complexity of models are the main bottlenecks to progress in efficient design, decision making under uncertainty, multiobjective design,fault-tolerant control and system monitoring. 
In this proposal we describe a radically new and different approach to complex systems which can short circuit the modeling and identification of the "plant", that is, the fixed part of the system, and directly proceed to design of the "controller", that is, the variable part, or that which is to be designed. The basic idea is to develop an approach wherein system measurements are directly processed strategically to extract the design parameters, rather than the current approach of first producing an identified complex model which is then analyzed to produce the design.  Our proposal describes the potential generalizations of these fundamental results and their exploitation in direct measurement or data-based design. Applications of this new approach to several areas are also briefly described and proposed for research: genomics, smart grid, adaptive control and circuits,hydraulic and gas networks, security monitoring and cyber security. Colaboração acadêmica USP-UP: controle para a optimização de recursos na exploração agrícola
Descrição: Este projeto tem como objectivo a formulação do problema de optimização de recursos numa exploração agrícola, considerando mais especificamente, através de estratégias de aplicação de herbicidas a taxa variada. A relevância deste problema deve-se não só à necessidade de minimizar recursos (pessoas e materiais), mas também à minimizar o impacto ambiental e promover o mais possível o equilíbrio ecológico. A execução do projeto requer a exploração das complementaridades entre as equipas da USP e da UP quer em expertise, meios para a abordagem aos problemas. Por outro lado, o facto de partilharem métodos matemáticos necessários para os desenvovimentos teóricos tornará a colaboração mais fácil. Importa referir a experiência da USP na aplicação de teoria de controle e de optimização a problemas no domínio da agricultura, bem como os contactos neste domínio. Os deslocamentos dos pesquisadores da UP à USP e da USP à UP servirão, no primeiro ano, para uma especificação ainda mais detalhada das actividades e para ?brainstorming?, e num segundo ano, para análise e discussão detalhada dos resultados obtidos. Internet e skype servirão de base para uma interação persistente durante a execução das atividades.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. 2011 - 2013
Utilização de controle fuzzy para a regulação da potência/tensão de um grupo gerador-motor a biodiesel operando interligado à rede de distribuição
Descrição: Este projeto tem por objetivo investigar os efeitos de um grupo motor-gerador (GMG) a biodiesel quando opera conectado à rede de distribuição e propor uma estratégia de controle para o fluxo de potência (ativa/torque e reativa) e para a amplitude da tensão terminal e freqüência do GMG. Será investigada, também, a limitação do uso de controle monovariável baseado em uma malha que regula a potência ativa produzida pelo gerador e outra, que regula a excitação do GMG. A operação interligada utilizará um alimentador padrão IEEE-1547 e um set-up experimental que será conectado à rede trifásica do Laboratório de Fontes Alternativas e Processamento de Energia (LAFAPE). Para avaliar o desempenho do sistema de controle do GMG, o mesmo será submetido a condições adversas de carregamento (cargas não-lineares, desequilibradas, etc.) e de combustível (diesel ou biodiesel) além da operação sob contingência (isolada da rede de distribuição). Espera-se com este projeto contribuir para o desenvolvimento tecnológico e a formação de mestres e doutores para o desenvolvimento de pesquisas avançadas na área de controle e processamento de energia com fontes alternativas, uma vez que, soluções para a produção de energia a partir de fontes alternativas têm sido foco de investimento e discussões por parte de governos e universidades.. 2003 - 2005
Estratégia de controle fuzzy para uma classe de sistemas não lineares incertos
Descrição: O presente projeto tem como objetivo o uso da modelagem fuzzy T-S para o controle de sistemas dinâmicos não-lineares incertos usando funções de Lyapunov quadráticas por parte para obter controladores mais eficientes e menos conservadores considerando a natureza de ganho programado dos modelos fuzzy T-S. O sistema não-linear é descrito por um modelo incerto em regiões em torno de pontos de equilíbrio e as regiões de atração estimadas a partir de funções de Lyapunov associadas aos sistemas lineares incertos. No presente projeto busca-se uma solução com chaveamentos mais suaves e com um número menor de controladores. O projeto compõe-se de investigação teórica sobre o problema de estabilização de sistemas não-lineares conjugado a trabalho experimental de laboratório em implementação de controladores em plataforma de sistemas em tempo real e será desenvolvido por participantes do grupo CNPQ Sistemas de Controle..
